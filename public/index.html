Carpal Tunnel Center

Endoscopic Carpal Tunnel Release

This is a minimal invasive procedure. In the endoscopic carpal tunnel release, the surgeon makes a small incision in the folds of the wrist and guides a thin tube containing a telescope attached to a camera directly to the surgical site, where he cuts the ligament forming the roof the carpal tunnel and releases pressure on the median nerve. No splinting is required, just a bulky bandage.

Our process

The efficient process that we offer includes:

Consultation with Dr. Green at his office or by videoconference.
(We also offer initial screening at our satellite office in Lakewood NJ.)

Arrangement at the same location, for electrical testing to confirm the diagnosis and establish objective baseline.

Surgical treatment with the single port endoscopic technique at our Ohio State licensed Surgery Center also located at the same location.

Postop follow-up at the same location with hand therapy provided by our licensed Occupational Therapist, also at the same location.

This process allows for rapid diagnosis and treatment with relief from symptoms. It also prevents further damage to the nerve caused by delay.
